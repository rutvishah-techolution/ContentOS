# Instagram Storyline — 18 diagnoses in cases everyone had given up on.

**Author:** Doctor  ·  **Angle:** The outcome/case

**Hook:** 376 rare disease cases. Every one of them a dead end. Families told there was nothing left to try, files closed, no name for what was wrong. Then a team at Boston Children's Hospital ran those same files through AI—and found 18 answers that had been sitting in the data the whole time.
**Villain:** The industry has poured $30-40 billion into generative AI, and 95% of pilots deliver zero measurable P&L impact. Most of that money chases model quality when the real problem is that the AI was never pointed at a workflow that mattered. A pilot that impresses in a demo does nothing for the patient in the queue.
**Shift:** The Boston Children's result did not come from a bigger model. It came from aiming a capable model at a specific, painful operational reality—376 unsolved cases—and pairing every AI output with expert clinical review before it counted. The win was in the sequencing, not the technology.
**Hero:** The insight: AI delivers when it is anchored to a workflow the organization already knows is broken, and when human expertise stays in the loop to confirm what the machine surfaces. Boston Children's used OpenAI's Deep Research model with Harvard, then ran every candidate diagnosis through expert review. 18 new diagnoses—a 4.8% additional yield on cases that were closed. And it scales down: integrating AI into existing health records runs $7,800 to $10,400, not a moonshot budget.
**Proof:** 18 confirmed diagnoses out of 376 abandoned cases. Alongside it, AI-supported hospitals report 42% fewer diagnostic errors than non-AI facilities. These are not projections. They are outcomes measured against a specific operational baseline—cases that had a name and a status before the AI touched them, and a different status after.
**Learning:** AI does not find answers in cases nobody worked. It finds them in the cases you point it at.
**CTA:** The hard part is knowing which closed files are worth reopening. That is the diagnosis we run before any deployment—mapping an enterprise's own operational reality to find where AI actually pays.

# Interviewer — Raw Research

## Summary

The current enterprise AI landscape is marked by a significant tension: while agentic AI promises a 300% surge in adoption and the ability for startups to disrupt incumbents by automating complex workflows, many large organizations are struggling to translate this into measurable ROI. The initial 'tokenmaxxing' enthusiasm is giving way to a cost-conscious reality, exposing a critical gap where enterprises are failing to capture expected value from digital investments due to misaligned strategies, and are now facing the risks of 'rented intelligence' and the unexpected limitations of multi-agent coordination.

## Findings

### 1. Enterprise adoption of AI agents is projected to increase by 300% within the next two years.

- **Evidence:** MIT Technology Review report, June 10, 2026, stating a 300% surge in AI agent adoption is expected in two years.
- **Source:** https://www.technologyreview.com/2026/06/09/1137830/learning-to-lead-in-a-hybrid-human-ai-enterprise/
- **Why it matters:** This rapid acceleration in agent adoption means organizational design and operational workflows must fundamentally adapt. The core technical reality is that the enterprise structure itself is shifting, and leadership needs to understand the implications for task coordination and workforce restructuring, moving beyond hypothetical discussions to operational planning.

### 2. Agentic AI capabilities are enabling startups to compete at a scale previously reserved for large enterprises, thereby eroding the competitive moats of established companies.

- **Evidence:** Harvard Business Review analysis, May 31, 2026, detailing how agentic AI 'supercharges startups' and 'threatens incumbents'.
- **Source:** https://hbr.org/2026/07/how-agentic-ai-supercharges-startups-and-threatens-incumbents
- **Why it matters:** This indicates a critical structural shift in market dynamics. The operational complexity that once protected incumbents is being neutralized by autonomous workflows. Decision-makers must assess how their existing competitive advantages, often built on scale and process, are being undermined by these new technical capabilities, and how quickly they must adapt.

### 3. Many enterprises are struggling to realize AI ROI, leading to a 'tokenmaxxing' backlash where initial high AI usage costs are colliding with a lack of measurable business impact.

- **Evidence:** NEA partner Tiffany Luck's observations (June 18, 2026), citing Uber exceeding its annual AI budget and Meta discontinuing its internal AI usage leaderboard.
- **Source:** https://techcrunch.com/video/neas-tiffany-luck-says-entereprises-are-still-figuring-out-their-ai-roi/
- **Why it matters:** This exposes a fundamental disconnect between perceived AI value and actual financial return. The 'GenAI Paradox' is not merely a budget issue, but a strategic one: broad adoption without clear, quantifiable benefits is unsustainable. This necessitates a precise mapping of AI investments to operational outcomes, moving beyond speculative assertions to verifiable impact.

### 4. Organizations relying heavily on third-party AI models face significant risks including vendor lock-in, concentration risk, and potential business continuity threats.

- **Evidence:** KPMG's warning, May 31, 2026, against 'rented intelligence' and its strategic dependencies.
- **Source:** https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMingFBVV95cUxOR3hYZzAydlBDUmRmS2poY0VobEdVZjNOcXA5ZmZOak5DVXZZS1RQZ21VWmdaaVVYX2xPLWVBQm1uT09tX3pKcTh6S3UwbFJmdTl5cld6LXlrbmlmd3d4ODB2Yl90SkFVSTkzS1ZYSmtJanpTREtnNGRobWctSXMyd0syRm5YeDF6dHFZakVYdHpjUEZwdUJGZERzaTdtVU0?oc=1
- **Why it matters:** This highlights a critical governance and architectural decision. Outsourcing core intelligence capabilities creates a strategic vulnerability. Boards must understand the long-term implications of ceding control over foundational AI infrastructure, ensuring a balance between leveraging external innovation and maintaining internal strategic autonomy.

### 5. AI coding agents demonstrate a critical limitation in multi-agent coordination, with two agents performing worse than a single agent in collaborative tasks.

- **Evidence:** Stanford HAI research, June 13, 2026, on AI coding agents failing at teamwork.
- **Source:** https://hai.stanford.edu/news/ai-coding-agents-fail-at-teamwork
- **Why it matters:** This provides a crucial technical reality check for enterprises planning complex agentic architectures. The assumption that 'more agents equal better outcomes' is demonstrably false in certain contexts. This requires a deeper understanding of agentic system design, specifically around coordination mechanisms, to avoid performance degradation and ensure effective automation.

### 6. Organizations capture less than one-third of the expected value from digital investments when they prioritize technology capabilities over customer needs and redesigning workflows.

- **Evidence:** McKinsey research cited by MIT Technology Review, May 11, 2026, regarding value shortfalls from digital investments.
- **Source:** https://www.technologyreview.com/2026/05/11/1136967/fostering-breakthrough-ai-innovation-through-customer-back-engineering/
- **Why it matters:** This exposes a fundamental flaw in how many enterprises approach AI integration. Simply bolting AI onto existing, inefficient processes will not yield significant returns. The emphasis must shift to 'customer-back engineering' and a re-evaluation of the entire workflow, aligning AI strategy with tangible business and customer outcomes rather than technological adoption for its own sake.
